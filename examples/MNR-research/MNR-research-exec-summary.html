T1: 10-Q Q1 2026; 10-K FY2025

Thesis

MNR rolls up mature, low-decline conventional oil and gas (Anadarko core, plus Permian and San Juan added in two simultaneous Sep 2025 deals totaling ~$1.2B) and distributes substantially all free cash to unitholders. T1: 10-K FY2025 Item 1

The market is buying a high-teens distribution yield (annualized Q1 2026 rate ~$2.56/unit at a flagged ~$13–15 unit price). What you are actually underwriting is whether that cash coupon holds: it is ~70% gas by volume (Q1 2026), fully floating-rate on debt, hedged only partway out, and the payout is the residual after capex and interest — so it absorbs commodity weakness directly. The bull needs gas to hold and the borrowing base to stay put; the bear gets a coupon cut on the next leg down. T1: 8-K 2026-05-07; price is FLAG

Variant View / Where the Model Breaks

Q1 2026 declared coverage was ~1.0x ($107.4M CAD vs ~$107.7M declared) and the Q4→Q1 step-up from $0.53 to $0.64/unit left essentially no cushion. The single data point that flips the thesis is the October 2026 borrowing-base redetermination: if reserve value compresses on lower price decks and the redetermined base falls toward the $1.14B drawn, liquidity and the distribution both tighten at once. T1: 10-Q Q1 2026 MD&A

The $90.4M Deep Anadarko impairment plus the drilling pause is an early tell that the legacy core underperformed — exactly the kind of reserve disappointment that pressures a redetermination. Key Risks (Ranked by Materiality)

  1. Distribution-coverage / commodity pass-through. ~1.0x declared coverage; no minimum distribution; payout is the residual. A sustained gas or oil decline cuts the coupon directly (precedent: $0.79→$0.27 inside FY2025). T1: 10-K FY2025; 10-Q Q1 2026
  2. Borrowing-base redetermination (Oct 2026). Lower price decks or negative reserve revisions shrink the base toward the $1.14B drawn. Sep 2025 amendment carved out up to $750M of BB-reduction debt as a buffer, but this is the structural pressure point. T1: 10-Q Q1 2026 debt note
  3. Gas concentration + below-market IKAV contract. ~70% gas by volume (Q1 2026); inherited a firm sale of ~282 Bbtu through 2030 at a fixed $1.72/MMBtu, well below the ~$3.51 hedge strip — a locked revenue drag. T1: 8-K 2026-05-07 (production mix); 10-Q Q1 2026 Note 10 (IKAV commitment)
  4. Fully floating-rate debt. No rate hedges; 100bp on ~$1.14B is ~$11.5M/yr of CAD. Effective rate 7.7%. T1: 10-K FY2025 interest rate risk
  5. Integration of three basins in <18 months plus ongoing purchase-price adjustments and the Deep Anadarko write-down. Execution risk, not solvency. T1: 10-K FY2025

Analytical stance: pass. The equity is a levered, variable commodity coupon, not a mispriced credit or a special situation. No public debt to underwrite. MNR offers a high-teens distribution yield on a cash-generative but fully commodity-and-rate-exposed MLP whose payout is the first thing to get cut, into a soft gas tape, with acquisition-counterparty secondary selling and a $100M ATM as overhang. That is a yield trade with coverage already at ~1.0x, not a research-driven mispricing. EDGAR alone cannot close the valuation (no price, no peer multiples, no credit rating).

Re-underwrite only if one of these fires:

  • 1 October 2026 borrowing-base redetermination — if the base holds or rises despite a lower price deck, the distribution durability thesis strengthens materially.
  • 2 Unit-price dislocation — a move that prices the coupon for the downside (model the distribution at a stressed strip first; coverage is already ~1.0x). Pull Tier-2 inputs first: market price, peer EV/EBITDA and EV/flowing-Boe, any credit rating, FY2026 production guidance.

# MNR Exec Summary: Mach Natural Resources LP (NYSE: MNR)

EDGAR-only run (Tier 1 primary filings, no vendor data). Every number keeps a light source tag. Gaps are FLAGged, not papered over. Entity confirmed: CIK 0001980088, upstream oil and gas MLP, Oklahoma City. NOT the old Monmouth REIT.

## Key Insights

- MNR is a Tom Ward acquire-and-distribute MLP, not a growth compounder. It pays out essentially all available cash each quarter, so the unit is a levered, variable, commodity-price coupon: when prices fall, the distribution gets cut first (it already swung from $0.79 to $0.27/unit inside FY2025). [T1: 10-Q Q1 2026; 10-K FY2025]
- Cash generation is the real story and it is steadier than the headline. CFO held at ~$491-507M across FY2023-2025 even as the company doubled via acquisitions; Q1 2026 CFO was $170M (~$681M run-rate). The Q1 2026 GAAP net loss of $35M is a non-cash $103.8M mark-to-market hedge loss, not an operating problem. [T1: 10-Q Q1 2026 cash flow + EBITDA recon]
- The balance sheet is clean on maturity but unusual on structure: all debt sits under a single New Credit Agreement (revolving tranche up to $1.0B + term loan tranche up to $450M), Truist-agented, maturing Feb 27, 2029; $1.14B drawn as of 3/31/26, no bonds, no near-term wall, ~1.7-1.9x net leverage with covenant room to 3.0x. There is no fulcrum-security distressed angle here. [T1: 10-Q Q1 2026 Note 7; 10-K FY2025]
- The real risk is not a maturity, it is the borrowing base. RBL-only debt gets redetermined every April and October on reserve value; with oil spot below the ~$66 swap strikes in Q1 2026, the redetermination is the variable to watch, alongside a Q1 declared distribution coverage of only ~1.0x. [T1: cap-structure slice; 10-Q Q1 2026]
- This is a debt-instrument name with no public debt to buy. The only tradable security is the equity (the LP unit), and the equity is an apparent high-teens-yield commodity proxy using a flagged ~$13-15 unit price (not EDGAR-sourced), not a special situation. EDGAR gives us no market price, no peer multiples, no credit rating, no FY2026 production guidance (development capex budget $315-$360M is disclosed [T1: 10-K FY2025]): the valuation case cannot be closed on primary sources alone (FLAG).

## Thesis

MNR rolls up mature, low-decline conventional oil and gas (Anadarko core, plus Permian and San Juan added in two simultaneous Sep 2025 deals totaling ~$1.2B) and distributes substantially all free cash to unitholders. [T1: 10-K FY2025 Item 1] The market is buying a high-teens distribution yield (annualized Q1 2026 rate ~$2.56/unit against a ~$13-15 unit price). [T1: cap-structure slice; price is FLAG, not EDGAR] What you are actually underwriting is whether that cash coupon holds: it is ~70% gas by volume (Q1 2026 [T1: 8-K 2026-05-07]), fully floating-rate on debt, hedged only partway out, and the payout is the residual after capex and interest, so it absorbs commodity weakness directly. The bull needs gas to hold and the borrowing base to stay put; the bear gets a coupon cut on the next leg down in prices.

## Variant view / where the model breaks

The market is pricing MNR as a yield instrument and anchoring on the trailing distribution. The point of maximum fragility is the residual nature of that distribution combined with RBL mechanics. Q1 2026 declared coverage was ~1.0x CAD ($107.4M CAD vs ~$107.7M declared) and the $0.53 to $0.64 step-up left essentially no cushion. [T1: cap-structure slice; 10-Q Q1 2026] The single data point that flips the thesis is the October 2026 borrowing-base redetermination: if reserve value compresses on lower price decks and the redetermined base falls toward the $1.14B drawn, liquidity and the distribution both tighten at once. The $90.4M Deep Anadarko impairment plus the drilling pause is an early tell that the legacy core underperformed, which is exactly the kind of reserve disappointment that pressures a redetermination. [T1: 10-K FY2025; 8-K 2026-05-07] Secondary fragility: management is a forced seller of optionality. Tom Ward has 4.8M units pledged as personal debt collateral (Ward himself bought 153K units in the April offering), the IKAV and Sabinal acquisition counterparties (VEPU Inc., Simlog Inc., Sabinal Energy Operating) sold 9M units at $13.05 in April 2026, and a $100M ATM was opened in May 2026 -- all dilutive or overhang signals into a soft tape. [T1: catalysts slice; 424B4 2026-04-06; 8-K 2026-05-22]

## Key risks (ranked by materiality)

1. **Distribution-coverage / commodity pass-through.** ~1.0x declared coverage; no minimum distribution; payout is the residual. A sustained gas or oil decline cuts the coupon directly (precedent: $0.79 to $0.27 inside FY2025). This is the thesis. [T1: cap-structure; catalysts]
2. **Borrowing-base redetermination (Oct 2026).** RBL-only structure; lower price decks or negative reserve revisions shrink the base toward the drawn balance. The Sep 2025 amendment carved out up to $750M of BB-reduction debt as a buffer, but this is the structural pressure point. [T1: cap-structure slice]
3. **Gas concentration + below-market IKAV contract.** ~70% gas by volume as of Q1 2026; inherited a firm sale of ~282 Bbtu through 2030 at a fixed $1.72/MMBtu, well below the ~$3.51 hedge strip, a locked revenue drag on those volumes. [T1: 8-K 2026-05-07 press release (production mix); 10-Q Q1 2026 Note 10 (IKAV commitment)]
4. **Fully floating-rate debt.** No rate hedges; 100bp on ~$1.14B is ~$11.5M/yr of CAD. Effective rate 7.7%. [T1: industry-risks slice; 10-K FY2025]
5. **Integration of three basins in <18 months** plus ongoing purchase-price adjustments and the Deep Anadarko write-down. Execution, not solvency. [T1: 10-K FY2025]

## The read: pass at current setup; two triggers that would change it

Analytical stance: pass. The equity is a levered, variable commodity coupon, not a mispriced credit or a special situation. There is no public debt to underwrite. What MNR offers is a high-teens distribution yield on a cash-generative but fully commodity-and-rate-exposed MLP whose payout is the first thing to get cut, into a soft gas tape, with acquisition-counterparty secondary selling and a $100M ATM as overhang. That is a yield trade with coverage already at ~1.0x, not a research-driven mispricing. EDGAR alone cannot close the valuation (no price, no peer multiples, no credit rating).

The security to watch, not act on now. Two triggers that would warrant re-underwriting: (1) the October 2026 borrowing-base redetermination: if the base holds or rises on a lower price deck, the distribution durability thesis strengthens materially; (2) a unit-price dislocation that prices the coupon for the downside (model the distribution at a stressed strip first; coverage is already ~1.0x). Re-underwrite only after pulling the Tier-2 inputs EDGAR cannot give: market price, peer EV/EBITDA and EV/flowing-Boe, any credit rating, FY2026 production guidance, and the full quarterly distribution series. Note: FY2026 development capex budget ($315-$360M) is already EDGAR-sourced [T1: 10-K FY2025].
